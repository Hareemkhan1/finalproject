About Humber College

Humber College is a public college in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. It was founded in 1967 and has since become one of Canada's largest and most diverse colleges, offering over 200 certificate, diploma, and degree programs in a wide range of fields.

Over the years, Humber College has undergone significant growth and expansion, including the addition of new campuses and the development of new programs to meet the changing needs of students and the job market. Today, Humber College is a leading institution of higher learning in Canada and a valuable resource for students, employers, and the community.

Campuses

Humber College has three main campuses located in Toronto, Ontario, Canada:

  • North Campus: Located in the northwest part of Toronto, the North Campus is the largest of Humber College's campuses. It is home to many of the college's programs, including business, engineering, media studies, and technology.
  • Lakeshore Campus: Located on the shore of Lake Ontario, the Lakeshore Campus is known for its picturesque views and beautiful campus grounds. It is home to many of the college's programs, including hospitality, fashion, and performing arts.
  • Orangeville Campus: Located in Orangeville, Ontario, the Orangeville Campus is a smaller campus that focuses on providing hands-on training in areas such as sustainable energy and skilled trades.

Programs

Humber College offers over 200 certificate, diploma, and degree programs across a wide range of fields. Some of its most popular programs include:

  • Business: Humber College offers a variety of business programs, including accounting, marketing, human resources, and entrepreneurship. The college's business programs are known for their focus on practical, real-world experience, with opportunities for students to work on real projects with industry partners.
  • Media Studies: Humber College's media studies programs are among the most highly regarded in Canada, with a focus on hands-on learning and practical experience in areas such as journalism, broadcasting, and public relations.
  • Health Sciences: Humber College offers a variety of health sciences programs, including nursing, paramedic, and occupational therapy. These programs are known for their strong emphasis on clinical experience and practical skills development.
  • Technology: Humber College's technology programs are designed to prepare students for careers in fast-growing fields such as cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, and robotics. The college's technology programs are known for their focus on hands-on learning and practical experience.
